Hotpot in Sichuan China

Origin of hotpot

Here is a quote from Baidu's website:

四川火锅，以麻，辣，鲜，香著称，他来源于民间，升华于庙堂，无论是贩夫走卒、达官显宦、文人骚客、商贾农工，还是红男绿女、黄发垂髫，其消费群体涵盖之广泛、人均消费次数之大，都是他地望尘莫及的。 作为一种美食，火锅已成为四川和重庆两地的代表美食。

Translation: Sichuan hotpot is renowned for its spiciness, numbing sensation, freshness, and rich aroma. Originating from the common people and refined in the halls of power, it appeals to a wide range of consumers, including peddlers, officials, scholars, merchants, farmers, men, women, the young, and the elderly. Its broad consumer base and high frequency of consumption are unmatched. As a culinary delight, hotpot has become a representative dish of Sichuan and Chongqing.

Cooking method

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons Sichuan Doubanjiang (broad bean paste)
  • 100 grams beef tallow
  • 100 grams vegetable oil
  • 100 grams dried chili peppers
  • 50 grams Sichuan peppercorns
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 15 grams rock sugar
  • 1 small piece of old ginger
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 2 sections of Beijing scallion (or Chinese green onion)
  • 2 tablespoons white liquor (or Chinese baijiu)
  • 1 can bone broth
  • 1 piece dried tangerine peel
  • 2 pieces tsaoko (or black cardamom)

Hotpot cooking method Visit Baidu Sichuan Hptpot!

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pan, then add dried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns, and stir-fry until fragrant. Remove them and set aside. Add white sugar to the oil and cook over low heat until melted, then add the smashed scallion segments, ginger pieces, and garlic cloves.
  2. Once the scallion and garlic turn a light yellow color, add all the spices and stir-fry together. Then, add the Sichuan Doubanjiang and mix well. Pour in the white liquor, light soy sauce, and beef tallow, and stir-fry everything together.
  3. Finally, add the entire can of bone broth, add rock sugar, and return the previously fried chili peppers and Sichuan peppercorns to the pan. This completes the hotpot base.
